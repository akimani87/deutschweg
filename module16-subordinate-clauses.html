A2 · Sentence Structure

Subordinate
Clauses

A subordinate clause connects two ideas into one sentence. The four conjunctions that create them — weil, dass, wenn, ob — each trigger the same golden rule: the verb moves to the very end of the clause. This single rule is the most-tested sentence structure on the Goethe A2 exam.

Why this matters: Every A2 Schreiben task rewards you for connecting sentences. "Ich lerne Deutsch. Ich möchte arbeiten." = 2 simple sentences. "Ich lerne Deutsch, weil ich in Deutschland arbeiten möchte." = 1 complex sentence that scores higher. The examiner is looking exactly for this.

The golden rule: verb goes to the end

A subordinate clause is a second sentence that you attach to a main sentence using a conjunction. In English, the word order stays the same in both halves. In German, one rule changes everything: when a conjunction starts a subordinate clause, the verb in that clause moves to the very last position.

The golden rule:

MAIN CLAUSE — verb in position 2 as normal
SUBORDINATE CLAUSE (after weil / dass / wenn / ob) — verb goes to the END

Ich lerne Deutsch. + Ich möchte in Deutschland arbeiten.
→ Ich lerne Deutsch, weil ich in Deutschland arbeiten möchte.

The comma rule — always a comma before the conjunction
Always place a comma before weil, dass, wenn, and ob when they introduce a subordinate clause. No exceptions.

✗ Ich lerne Deutsch weil ich in Deutschland arbeiten möchte.
✓ Ich lerne Deutsch, weil ich in Deutschland arbeiten möchte.
The four A2 subordinating conjunctions
weil
BECAUSE · REASON
Gives the reason for something in the main clause.
..., weil ich müde bin.
..., weil er arbeiten muss.
dass
THAT · REPORTING
Reports what someone thinks, says, knows, or believes.
..., dass er morgen kommt.
..., dass es schwer ist.
wenn
WHEN / IF · CONDITION
Expresses a condition (if) or repeated time (when/whenever).
..., wenn ich Zeit habe.
..., wenn das Wetter gut ist.
ob
WHETHER / IF · INDIRECT ❓
Turns a yes/no question into an indirect question.
..., ob er kommt.
..., ob das stimmt (right).

weil — because

weil introduces the reason for the action or state in the main clause. After weil, the entire verb — including modals, separable prefixes, and Perfekt auxiliaries — moves to the very end of the clause.

weil word order:

weil + SUBJECT + [objects/adverbs] + VERB (end)

With modal: weil + SUBJECT + [middle] + INFINITIVE + MODAL (very end)

Ich lerne Deutsch, weil ich in Deutschland arbeiten möchte.
↑ modal verb goes last, infinitive just before it

More weil sentences — African context
WEIL
Fatima kauft das Kleid nicht, weil es zu teuer ist.
Fatima doesn't buy the dress because it is too expensive.
Negation in the main clause doesn't affect the weil-clause structure. Verb ist goes to the end.
WEIL + MODAL
Kwame geht zur Bibliothek, weil er für die Prüfung lernen muss.
Kwame goes to the library because he has to study for the exam.
muss (modal) goes to the very end. The infinitive lernen goes just before the modal.
WEIL + SEIN
Amina ist früh aufgestanden, weil sie einen frühen Bus nehmen musste.
Amina got up early because she had to take an early bus.
musste (past modal) goes to end. nehmen (infinitive) comes just before it.

dass — that

dass introduces a subordinate clause that reports what someone thinks, says, knows, or believes. In English you can often drop "that" — "I think (that) German is difficult." In German, dass cannot be omitted, and the verb always moves to the end.

dass — A2 Schreiben phrases ready to use
OPINION
Ich finde, dass Deutschland sehr interessant ist.
I find that Germany is very interesting.
Exam-ready opinion sentence. Use this exact structure in Schreiben when asked for your opinion.
BELIEF
Amina glaubt, dass sie die Prüfung besteht.
Amina believes that she will pass the exam.
glauben + dass. Verb besteht goes to end of dass-clause.
PITY
Es ist schade, dass Fatima nicht kommen kann.
It is a pity that Fatima cannot come.
Two verbs at the end: infinitive kommen + modal kann (modal last). Very natural A2 register.
KNOWLEDGE
Kwame weiß, dass er noch viel lernen muss.
Kwame knows that he still has a lot to learn.
wissen + dass. Modal muss goes to end, infinitive lernen just before it.

wenn — when / if

wenn has two uses: it can express a condition (if something is true…) or a repeated time (whenever/each time something happens…). Both use the same word order: verb goes to the end of the wenn-clause.

There is one additional rule unique to wenn: when the wenn-clause comes first in the sentence (before the main clause), the main clause verb moves to position 1 — directly after the comma. This is called inversion.

The wenn inversion rule:

Normal order: Ich lerne Deutsch, wenn ich Zeit habe.
Main clause first → wenn-clause after the comma → no inversion

Inverted: Wenn ich Zeit habe, lerne ich Deutsch.
wenn-clause first → comma → main clause VERB comes FIRST (before subject)
The inversion — wenn-clause in position 1
INVERSION: WENN-CLAUSE FILLS POSITION 1 → MAIN CLAUSE VERB COMES FIRST
Wenn ich Zeit habe,  lerne ich Deutsch.
[wenn-clause · verb at end] , [main verb first · then subject]
WENN-CLAUSE FIRST

,

wenn — condition (if)
CONDITION
Amina geht ins Kino, wenn sie Geld hat.
Amina goes to the cinema if she has money.
Main clause first → wenn-clause after comma → verb hat at end. No inversion needed here.
INVERTED · CONDITION
Wenn Kwame krank ist, bleibt er zu Hause.
If Kwame is sick, he stays home.
wenn-clause first → verb bleibt comes first in main clause, then subject er. Inversion in action.
wenn — repeated time (when/whenever)
REPEATED TIME
Wenn Kofi nach Hause kommt, kocht er Fufu.
When(ever) Kofi comes home, he cooks fufu.
Repeated habit. wenn-clause first → inversion: kocht er (verb before subject) in main clause.
REPEATED TIME
Fatima fühlt sich glücklich, wenn sie Musik hört.
Fatima feels happy when(ever) she listens to music.
Main clause first, no inversion. Verb hört goes to end of wenn-clause.

ob — whether / if

ob turns a direct yes/no question into an indirect question embedded inside a sentence. In English: "Is he coming?" → "I don't know whether he is coming." In German: "Kommt er?" → "Ich weiß nicht, ob er kommt." The same verb-to-end rule applies.

ob vs wenn:

ob = whether/if (indirect question from a YES/NO question)
wenn = when/if (condition or time — not a question)

"Kommt er?" → Ich frage, ob er kommt. (indirect ❓)
"Er kommt." → Ich bin froh, wenn er kommt. (condition — not a question)
Common verbs that introduce ob-clauses
VERBS THAT TYPICALLY TAKE OB

Direct question → indirect question with ob
DIRECT → OB
Kommt der Bus pünktlich? → Fatima fragt, ob der Bus pünktlich kommt.
Is the bus on time? → Fatima asks whether the bus is on time.
Direct question has verb first. ob-clause: verb goes to end. kommt → kommt (end).
DIRECT → OB
Hat Kwame Zeit? → Amina fragt, ob Kwame Zeit hat.
Does Kwame have time? → Amina asks whether Kwame has time.
hat moves from position 1 (direct question) to end (ob-clause).
OB + MODAL
Kofi weiß nicht, ob er die Prüfung bestehen kann.
Kofi doesn't know whether he can pass the exam.
Modal kann goes to end. Infinitive bestehen goes just before the modal.

Kwame, Amina, Kofi, Fatima — all four conjunctions

The #1 mistake for African English speakers writing A2 Schreiben: keeping the verb in position 2 after weil. "Ich lerne Deutsch, weil ich möchte in Deutschland arbeiten." This sounds natural in English but is penalised in every German exam. The verb must go to the END.

The 4 most common African learner mistakes
MISTAKE 1 · verb in pos.2 after weil
..., weil ich möchte arbeiten.
..., weil er hat keine Zeit.
CORRECT · verb at END after weil
..., weil ich arbeiten möchte.
..., weil er keine Zeit hat.
MISTAKE 2 · no comma before conjunction
Ich lerne Deutsch weil es wichtig ist.
Er sagt dass er kommt.
CORRECT · always a comma
Ich lerne Deutsch, weil es wichtig ist.
Er sagt, dass er kommt.
MISTAKE 3 · no inversion after wenn-first
Wenn ich Zeit habe, ich lerne.
Wenn er kommt, wir essen.
CORRECT · inversion after wenn-first
Wenn ich Zeit habe, lerne ich.
Wenn er kommt, essen wir.
MISTAKE 4 · ob used as wenn
Ich bin froh, ob er kommt.
Ich komme, ob ich Zeit habe.
CORRECT · wenn for conditions
Ich bin froh, wenn er kommt.
Ich komme, wenn ich Zeit habe.
All four conjunctions — African context paragraph
WEIL
Kwame lernt jeden Tag Deutsch, weil er in Berlin studieren möchte.
Kwame studies German every day because he wants to study in Berlin.
weil + modal möchte at end. Infinitive studieren goes just before the modal.
DASS
Amina denkt, dass die Deutschprüfung nicht so schwer ist.
Amina thinks that the German exam is not so difficult.
denken + dass. Verb ist goes to the end of the dass-clause.
WENN · INVERTED
Wenn Kofi die Prüfung besteht, feiert er mit seiner Familie.
When Kofi passes the exam, he will celebrate with his family.
wenn-clause first: besteht at end. Main clause inverted: feiert er (verb before subject).
OB
Fatima fragt, ob der Deutschkurs teuer ist.
Fatima asks whether the German course is expensive.
Indirect yes/no question. ob + subject + adjective + verb at end. Comma before ob.
Exam tip — Goethe A2 Schreiben:

The A2 writing task often asks you to: give a reason, express an opinion, describe a condition, or report something. These map directly to the four conjunctions:

✓ Give a reason → weil (because)
✓ Express opinion → Ich finde/denke/glaube, dass
✓ Describe condition → wenn (if/when)
✓ Express uncertainty → Ich weiß nicht, ob

Each of these scores under "grammatische Strukturen". Use at least two different conjunction types in your writing task.
What you now know:

✓ Subordinate clauses send the verb to the END — always
✓ Always use a comma before weil, dass, wenn, ob
✓ weil = because (reason) — most common in A2 Schreiben
✓ dass = that (opinion, belief, knowledge, reporting)
✓ wenn = when/if (condition or repeated time) — inversion if wenn-clause comes first
✓ ob = whether (indirect yes/no questions) — not to be confused with wenn
