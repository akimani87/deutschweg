Prepositions

Nine German prepositions — an, auf, hinter, in, neben, über, unter, vor, zwischen — can take either Akkusativ or Dativ depending on whether the sentence expresses movement toward or location. Getting this right separates A2 passers from those who lose marks on every single sentence involving place.

The memory trick: Ask yourself one question before choosing the case:

Wohin? (Where to? — something is moving) → Akkusativ  ·  A for Action
Wo? (Where? — something is staying) → Dativ  ·  D for Dwelling

The 9 two-way prepositions

Most German prepositions are fixed — they always take the same case. But these nine prepositions take either Akkusativ or Dativ depending on the meaning of the sentence. This is why they are called Wechselpräpositionen — switching prepositions.

an
auf
hinter
in
neben
über
unter
vor
zwischen
Memorise the 9 with this phrase:

an · auf · hinter · in · neben · über · unter · vor · zwischen

All nine describe position in space. They tell you where something is or where something is going.
The golden rule — one question, one answer
Wohin?
WHERE TO? · MOVEMENT
AKKUSATIV
Something is moving toward a place. The action creates a new position.
Ich gehe in die Schule.
Kwame legt das Buch auf den Tisch.
Die Katze springt unter das Bett.
Wo?
WHERE? · LOCATION
DATIV
Something is staying in a place. No movement, just position.
Ich bin in der Schule.
Das Buch liegt auf dem Tisch.
Die Katze schläft unter dem Bett.
Memory trick — A and D:

A for Action = Akkusativ  (something is happening, moving, changing)
D for Dwelling = Dativ  (something is staying put, resting, living there)
The contrast in one pair
WOHIN? · AKK
Amina geht in die Küche.
Amina goes into the kitchen.
She is moving. Wohin? → in + die (die Küche, feminine, Akk unchanged).
WO? · DAT
Amina kocht in der Küche.
Amina is cooking in the kitchen.
She is already there. Wo? → in + der (die Küche → der Küche in Dativ, feminine).
The trap: Both sentences use the same preposition in and the same noun Küche. The only thing that changes is the article — die (Akkusativ) or der (Dativ). This tiny word change carries all the meaning.

Wohin? vs Wo?

Before writing any sentence with a two-way preposition, ask yourself one question. That question will tell you which case to use. Train this as a reflex — it takes only a second and prevents every case error.

The two-step method:

Step 1 — Find the verb. Is it a movement verb or a position verb?
Step 2 — Ask: Wohin? (Where to?) → Akkusativ  ·  Wo? (Where?) → Dativ
Movement verbs → Akkusativ (Wohin?)
VERBS THAT SIGNAL MOVEMENT → ALWAYS AKKUSATIV
gehen  — to go  ·  kommen  — to come
fahren  — to drive/travel  ·  fliegen  — to fly
legen  — to lay/put (flat)  ·  stellen  — to place (upright)
hängen  — to hang (action)  ·  setzen  — to set/place
Position verbs → Dativ (Wo?)
VERBS THAT SIGNAL POSITION → ALWAYS DATIV
sein  — to be  ·  bleiben  — to stay/remain
liegen  — to lie/be lying  ·  stehen  — to stand/be standing
hängen  — to hang (state)  ·  sitzen  — to sit/be sitting
arbeiten  — to work  ·  wohnen  — to live (reside)
Note — hängen: This verb exists in two forms. hängen (weak, regular) = to hang something up → Wohin? → Akkusativ. hängen (strong, irregular — hing, gehangen) = to be hanging → Wo? → Dativ. Context always makes it clear.
Full sentence pairs — Wohin? vs Wo?
GEHEN · WOHIN?
Kwame geht in den Supermarkt.
Kwame goes into the supermarket.
gehen = movement. Wohin? → Akkusativ. der Supermarkt (masc) → den Supermarkt.
ARBEITEN · WO?
Kofi arbeitet in dem Büro.
Kofi works in the office.
arbeiten = position (stays there). Wo? → Dativ. das Büro (neut) → dem Büro → usually contracted: im Büro.
LEGEN · WOHIN?
Fatima legt das Buch auf den Tisch.
Fatima puts the book on the table.
legen = placing action. Wohin? → Akkusativ. der Tisch (masc) → den Tisch.
LIEGEN · WO?
Das Buch liegt auf dem Tisch.
The book is lying on the table.
liegen = position/state. Wo? → Dativ. der Tisch (masc) → dem Tisch.

Article changes & contractions

Once you know whether to use Akkusativ or Dativ, you need the correct article. In Dativ, all articles change. In Akkusativ, only masculine changes. And in everyday speech, some article combinations are almost always contracted into a single word.

Article changes — the full table
DEFINITE ARTICLE AFTER TWO-WAY PREPOSITIONS
GENDER NOMINATIV AKKUSATIV (Wohin?) DATIV (Wo?) CHANGES?
Masculinederdendem✦ both change
FemininediediederDat only
NeuterdasdasdemDat only
Pluraldiedieden (+n)Dat only
The key insight: In Akkusativ, only masculine changes (der → den). Feminine, neuter, and plural stay the same. That's why masculine is always the tricky one. In Dativ, all four change — der, die, das, die all become dem, der, dem, den.
Contractions — the four you must know
in + dem
im
Kofi ist im Büro.
Kofi is in the office.
DATIV
in + das
ins
Wir gehen ins Kino.
We go to the cinema.
AKKUSATIV
an + dem
am
Amina steht am Markt.
Amina stands at the market.
DATIV
an + das
ans
Kwame geht ans Fenster.
Kwame goes to the window.
AKKUSATIV
Contraction rule: In everyday German, in dem and an dem are almost always contracted. Writing "in dem Büro" instead of "im Büro" is grammatically correct but sounds unnatural and will be marked as awkward on the exam. Use the contracted form whenever possible.

Other contractions exist (auf das → aufs, vor das → vors, etc.) but im, ins, am, ans are the four you must know for A2.
Contractions in context — both cases of "in"
INS · IN + DAS · AKK
Fatima geht ins Krankenhaus.
Fatima goes to the hospital.
das Krankenhaus (neuter). Movement → Akkusativ → in + das = ins.
IM · IN + DEM · DAT
Fatima arbeitet im Krankenhaus.
Fatima works in the hospital.
das Krankenhaus (neuter). Location → Dativ → in + dem = im.

Most tested A2 combinations

Certain preposition + article combinations appear again and again in A2 Hören and Lesen texts. Recognising them instantly — without stopping to analyse case — saves you valuable time in the exam.

Fixed location phrases — Dativ (Wo?)
HIGH-FREQUENCY LOCATION PHRASES — ALWAYS DATIV
im Büro  — in the office  ·  im Supermarkt  — in the supermarket
im Restaurant  — in the restaurant  ·  im Krankenhaus  — in the hospital
am Markt  — at the market  ·  am Bahnhof  — at the train station
auf dem Tisch  — on the table  ·  auf dem Markt  — at the market
in der Schule  — in/at school  ·  in der Stadt  — in the city
an der Tür  — at the door  ·  vor dem Haus  — in front of the house
Movement phrases — Akkusativ (Wohin?)
HIGH-FREQUENCY MOVEMENT PHRASES — ALWAYS AKKUSATIV
ins Kino  — to the cinema  ·  ins Büro  — into the office
ins Krankenhaus  — to the hospital  ·  ans Meer  — to the sea
in die Schule  — to school  ·  in die Stadt  — into the city
auf den Markt  — to the market  ·  an den See  — to the lake
Exam tip: In A2 Hören, the speaker will describe where people are going and where things are located. The moment you hear geht / fährt / kommt before a preposition — Akkusativ. The moment you hear ist / bleibt / arbeitet / wohnt / liegt / steht — Dativ. Train your ear to catch the verb first.
Full exam-style sentences
IM · DATIV
Kofi arbeitet jeden Tag im Büro.
Kofi works in the office every day.
arbeiten = position verb. das Büro → in + dem = im. Dativ.
INS · AKKUSATIV
Am Freitag gehen wir ins Kino.
On Friday we go to the cinema.
gehen = movement verb. das Kino → in + das = ins. Akkusativ.
AM · DATIV
Amina kauft Gemüse am Markt.
Amina buys vegetables at the market.
kaufen here = position at the market. der Markt → an + dem = am. Dativ.
IN DIE · AKKUSATIV
Kwame fährt jeden Morgen in die Stadt.
Kwame drives into the city every morning.
fahren = movement verb. die Stadt (feminine) → in die Stadt. Akkusativ unchanged.

Kwame, Amina, Kofi, Fatima — market, office, school

Two-way prepositions describe everyday life: going to school, being at the market, working in the office. Here are real A2-level sentences built around African daily routines — the kind that appear in A2 exam texts.

A day in Lagos — full routine
WOHIN? · AKK
Um 7 Uhr fährt Kwame ins Büro.
At 7 o'clock Kwame drives to the office.
fährt = movement. das Büro → ins. Akkusativ.
WO? · DAT
Er arbeitet bis 17 Uhr im Büro.
He works in the office until 5pm.
arbeitet = position. das Büro → im. Dativ.
WOHIN? · AKK
Amina geht auf den Markt.
Amina goes to the market.
geht = movement. der Markt (masc) → auf den Markt. Akkusativ.
WO? · DAT
Sie kauft frisches Gemüse auf dem Markt.
She buys fresh vegetables at the market.
kauft = position. der Markt (masc) → auf dem Markt. Dativ.
WOHIN? · AKK
Kofi bringt das Essen in die Schule.
Kofi brings the food to school.
bringt = movement/direction. die Schule (fem) → in die Schule. Akkusativ unchanged.
WO? · DAT
Fatima lernt jeden Tag in der Bibliothek.
Fatima studies in the library every day.
lernt = position. die Bibliothek (fem) → in der Bibliothek. Dativ.
African learner mistakes — wrong and right
✗ WRONG
Ich gehe in der Schule.
✓ CORRECT
Ich gehe in die Schule.

gehen = movement → Wohin? → Akkusativ. die Schule stays die in Akkusativ (feminine). Using der (Dativ) here is the most common African learner error.

✗ WRONG
Das Buch liegt auf den Tisch.
✓ CORRECT
Das Buch liegt auf dem Tisch.

liegen = position → Wo? → Dativ. der Tisch → dem Tisch. Using den (Akkusativ masculine) with a position verb is wrong.

✗ WRONG
Kofi ist in das Büro.
✓ CORRECT
Kofi ist im Büro.

sein = position → Dativ → in + dem = im. "in das" signals movement — you can only use it with verbs like gehen, fahren, kommen.

✗ WRONG
Wir gehen im Kino.
✓ CORRECT
Wir gehen ins Kino.

gehen = movement → Akkusativ → in + das = ins. Using im (Dativ contraction) with a movement verb is wrong — im means you are already there.

Final exam summary:

1. Learn the 9 prepositions: an, auf, hinter, in, neben, über, unter, vor, zwischen
2. Ask the question: Wohin? → Akkusativ (A for Action) · Wo? → Dativ (D for Dwelling)
3. Know the contractions: im, ins, am, ans
4. In Akkusativ, only masculine changes: der → den. Feminine and neuter stay the same.
5. In Dativ, all change: der/die/das → dem/der/dem
