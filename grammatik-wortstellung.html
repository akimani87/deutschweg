German Word Order (Satzstruktur)

German has strict rules about where the verb goes. Once you understand the pattern, it becomes second nature.

Rule 1 — The Verb is ALWAYS in Position 2
Pos. 1
Subject or time
anything
Pos. 2 ⚡
VERB
always here!
Middle
subject / objects
other parts
End
2nd verb
infinitive / past participle
Ich lerne jeden Tag Deutsch.
I learn German every day. — normal order
Jeden Tag lerne ich Deutsch.
Every day I learn German. — "jeden Tag" moved to pos.1, verb stays at pos.2
Rule 2 — Modal + Infinitive → Verb Stays at End
Ich kann gut Deutsch sprechen.
I can speak German well. — kann at pos.2, infinitive at end
Amina muss morgen arbeiten.
Amina must work tomorrow. — same pattern
Rule 3 — Subordinate Clause (weil, dass, wenn…) → Verb at End
Ich lerne Deutsch, weil ich nach Deutschland gehen möchte.
I learn German because I want to go to Germany. — after weil, verb goes to end
💡 Key conjunctions that send the verb to the end: weil (because), dass (that), wenn (when/if), obwohl (although), damit (so that), bevor (before)
✏️ Quick Check

Ich lerne Deutsch, weil ich nach Deutschland gehen ___. (modal at the end?)
