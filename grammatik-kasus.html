The Four German Cases

Cases show the role a noun plays in a sentence. This is what the Goethe exam tests heavily — learn the patterns, not just the table.

Article Changes by Case
Case Question Masculine Feminine Neuter Plural
Nominativ
Who / What?
 Subject der die das die
Akkusativ
Whom / What?
 Direct object den die das die
Dativ
To whom?
 Indirect object dem der dem den
Genitiv
Whose?
 Possession des der des der
Real Sentences — Each Case in Action
Der Mann arbeitet. (NOM)
The man works. → der Mann is the subject (Nominativ)
Amina kauft den Bus. (AKK)
Amina buys the bus. → den Bus is the direct object (Akkusativ) — masc. changes!
Kwame gibt dem Kind ein Buch. (DAT)
Kwame gives the child a book. → dem Kind is the indirect object (Dativ)
Das ist das Haus des Mannes. (GEN)
This is the man's house. → des Mannes shows possession (Genitiv)
💡 Only masculine changes in Akkusativ: der → den. This is the #1 Goethe exam trap. Feminine, neuter and plural stay the same from Nominativ to Akkusativ.
