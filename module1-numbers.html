Numbers 20 to 1000 — the backwards pattern

English says "twenty-three" — tens first. German says "dreiundzwanzig" — literally three-and-twenty. Units come first, then "und", then tens. All as one word.

The pattern: [units] + und + [tens] — one word, no spaces

23 = dreiundzwanzig  ·  45 = fünfundvierzig  ·  78 = achtundsiebzig

The trap that costs the most marks in Hören

The examiners use the teen/tens confusion deliberately. Tap each pair and listen for the difference. The key is the ending: -zehn has a final N, -zig does not.

Listen for: -zehn → ends in -TSAYN (hear the N)  ·  -zig → ends in -TSIG (no N)

Days, months and seasons — all masculine

All days of the week, all months, and all seasons are masculine (der) in German. One rule covers all three categories — no exceptions.

One rule: der Montag · der Januar · der Frühling — always der.

Ordinal numbers — first, second, third

Ordinal numbers are used for dates, floors, and rankings. Three are irregular — memorise only those. From 4th onwards the pattern is completely regular.

The 3 Irregulars: 1st = erst-  ·  2nd = zweit-  ·  3rd = dritt-
From 4th onwards: add -t to base (viert-, fünft-, zehnt-). From 20th: add -st (zwanzigst-).
