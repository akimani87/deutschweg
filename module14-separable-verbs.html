A2 · Verb Structure

Separable &
Inseparable Verbs

German verbs can be extended with prefixes that completely change their meaning. The critical distinction: some prefixes separate from the verb in a sentence (sending the prefix to the end), while others stay permanently attached. Both types appear constantly in the Goethe A2 Hören and Lesen tasks — and getting word order wrong in separable verbs is one of the most penalised errors in A2 Schreiben.

The single most important fact: In a main clause, a separable verb splits — the stem stays in position 2, but the prefix flies to the very end of the sentence. Forgetting this is the #1 word order mistake at A2 level.

What are separable verbs?

A separable verb is a verb built from a base verb + a short prefix. The prefix adds a specific meaning. When you use the verb in a sentence, the two parts split: the base verb stays in position 2, and the prefix jumps to the very end of the sentence.

Think of it like a sentence with a delayed punch — the prefix arrives last and completes the meaning.

Word order — prefix to the end

In a main clause, the stem of the separable verb goes to position 2 (right after the subject, as always), and the prefix travels to the very last position in the sentence. Everything else — the object, adverbs, prepositional phrases — stays in the middle.

Main clause — the standard pattern
AUFMACHEN — "Amina opens the window"
POS.1
Amina
POS.2 · STEM
macht
MIDDLE
das Fenster
END · PREFIX
auf
ABFAHREN — "The bus departs at 8 o'clock"
POS.1
Der Bus
POS.2 · STEM
fährt
MIDDLE
um 8 Uhr
END · PREFIX
ab
Questions — stem still in position 2 (or position 1 for yes/no)
W-QUESTION
Wann fährt der Zug ab?
When does the train depart?
W-word takes position 1 → stem in position 2 → prefix still goes to the end.
YES/NO QUESTION
Rufst du mich an?
Are you calling me? / Will you call me?
In yes/no questions, stem moves to position 1. Prefix still last.
IMPERATIVE
Hör bitte zu!
Please listen! (to someone)
In imperatives, stem comes first. Prefix still goes to the end.
Negation — nicht goes before the prefix
NICHT + PREFIX
Kofi ruft heute nicht an.
Kofi is not calling today.
nicht sits directly before the prefix at the end: …nicht an. This is the standard negation position for separable verbs.
The most penalised mistake in A2 Schreiben: leaving the prefix attached to the stem in position 2.

✗ Ich aufmache die Tür. — prefix stays attached → WRONG
✓ Ich mache die Tür auf. — stem in position 2, prefix at END → CORRECT

Inseparable verbs — prefixes that never leave

Some prefixes are permanently glued to the verb. They never separate, no matter the sentence type. The verb always moves as one complete unit to position 2 and stays there. These are called inseparable verbs (untrennbare Verben).

There are eight inseparable prefixes. If you see any of these at the start of a verb, you know it will never split:

be–
ACTION ON
besuchen (visit)
bestellen (order)
bezahlen (pay)
ge–
COMPLETION
gefallen (please/like)
gehören (belong to)
gelingen (succeed)
er–
RESULT / END
erklären (explain)
erlauben (allow)
erhalten (receive)
ver–
WRONG / CHANGE
verstehen (understand)
verkaufen (sell)
vergessen (forget)
zer–
APART / DESTROY
zerbrechen (break apart)
zerstören (destroy)
zerreißen (tear up)
ent–
AWAY / REMOVE
entscheiden (decide)
entfernen (remove)
entlassen (dismiss)
emp–
RECEIVE
empfehlen (recommend)
empfangen (receive)
empfinden (feel)
miss–
WRONG / FAIL
missverstehen (misunderstand)
misslingen (fail)
missbrauchen (misuse)
Inseparable verbs in sentences — the whole verb stays in position 2
VERSTEHEN · INSEP
Fatima versteht Deutsch sehr gut.
Fatima understands German very well.
verstehen is inseparable (ver–). The whole word stays in position 2. Nothing moves to the end.
BESUCHEN · INSEP
Kwame besucht seine Familie in Accra.
Kwame visits his family in Accra.
besuchen is inseparable (be–). Conjugated as one unit in position 2.
ERKLÄREN · INSEP
Die Lehrerin erklärt die Grammatik.
The teacher explains the grammar.
erklären is inseparable (er–). Stays together.
The key difference — side by side:

aufmachen (SEPARABLE): Ich mache die Tür auf. ← prefix splits to end
besuchen (INSEPARABLE): Ich besuche meine Mutter. ← stays together, nothing moves
Some prefixes are ambiguous! A handful of prefixes (über, unter, um, durch, wieder) can be separable OR inseparable depending on the verb and its meaning. The best strategy: learn each specific verb individually. If in doubt, a dictionary will mark it as trennbar (separable) or untrennbar (inseparable).

Perfekt sentences in context
SEP · HABEN
Amina hat Lebensmittel eingekauft.
Amina went grocery shopping. (lit. bought in groceries)
einkaufen is separable, no movement → haben. ein+ge+kauf+t = eingekauft. Participle at end.
INSEP · HABEN
Kofi hat die Aufgabe verstanden.
Kofi understood the task.
verstehen is inseparable (ver–). Perfekt participle: verstanden. No ge– added.

Separable verbs with modal verbs

When a separable verb is used together with a modal verb, the separable verb appears at the end of the sentence as an infinitive — and it stays together as one word. The prefix does not separate because the whole separable verb is being used as an infinitive.

The rule with modals: Modal in position 2 → separable verb at end as infinitive → prefix stays attached.

Ich muss morgen früh aufstehen.
I have to get up early tomorrow.

The infinitive aufstehen goes to the end complete — NOT "Ich muss morgen früh stehen auf."

More modal + separable examples
KÖNNEN + SEP
Kwame kann gut zuhören.
Kwame can listen well.
zuhören = separable, but used as infinitive after modal → stays together at end.
MÜSSEN + SEP
Der Zug muss jetzt abfahren.
The train has to depart now.
abfahren as infinitive after müssen → stays together. Not "…fahren ab".
WOLLEN + SEP
Amina will Deutsch anfangen.
Amina wants to start German.
anfangen = separable. As infinitive after modal, it stays as one unit at end.
WRONG · prefix splits with modal
Ich muss stehen auf.
Er kann rufen an.
Sie will kaufen ein.
CORRECT · whole infinitive at end
Ich muss aufstehen.
Er kann anrufen.
Sie will einkaufen.

Kwame, Amina, Kofi, Fatima — all patterns in context

All four patterns you have learned — present tense split, questions, Perfekt, and modal verbs — demonstrated in sentences set in African and German daily life.

PRESENT · SPLIT
Kwame steht jeden Morgen um sechs Uhr auf.
Kwame gets up at six every morning.
aufstehen: stem steht in pos. 2, prefix auf at end. Adverb "jeden Morgen um sechs Uhr" stays in middle.
PRESENT · QUESTION
Wann fängt der Deutschkurs an?
When does the German course start?
anfangen: wann takes pos.1, fängt in pos.2, an at the end.
PERFEKT · SEPARABLE
Amina hat gestern ihre Schwester angerufen.
Amina called her sister yesterday.
anrufen → angerufen. haben (not movement). Participle at end of sentence.
PERFEKT · INSEPARABLE
Kofi hat endlich die Prüfung bestanden.
Kofi finally passed the exam.
bestehen is inseparable (be–). Perfekt participle: bestanden. No ge– added.
MODAL + SEPARABLE
Fatima möchte ihre Kollegen zum Essen einladen.
Fatima would like to invite her colleagues to dinner.
einladen with möchten: infinitive stays together at end. Perfect polite exam register.
NEGATION
Kwame macht das Fenster nicht auf.
Kwame is not opening the window.
nicht comes directly before the prefix: …nicht auf. This is the fixed position for negating separable verbs.

Exam tip — Goethe A2 Hören and Lesen:

Separable verbs appear in almost every A2 listening and reading text. When you hear or read a sentence, listen for:

✓ A short word at the very end of the sentence that seems to "complete" the verb → that's a separated prefix
✓ Common ones in exam texts: an, aus, auf, ab, ein, mit, zu

In Schreiben, always double-check that your separable verb prefix is at the END of the sentence — not attached to the stem in position 2.
What you now know:

✓ Separable verbs split: stem in pos. 2, prefix goes to END of sentence
✓ The 9 separable prefixes: auf, an, aus, ein, mit, nach, vor, zu, ab
✓ The 8 inseparable prefixes: be, ge, er, ver, zer, ent, emp, miss — never separate
✓ Perfekt of separable verbs: PREFIX + ge + STEM + t/en (aufgemacht, angerufen)
✓ Perfekt of inseparable verbs: no ge– added (besucht, verstanden)
✓ With modal verbs: the separable verb stays as one infinitive at the end
✓ Negation: nicht goes directly before the prefix (…nicht auf)
